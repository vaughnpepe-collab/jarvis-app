# 00 — Disclaimer & How To Use This Course

## Read this first

This course and the accompanying Expert Advisor are **educational material**.
They are **not** financial, investment, or trading advice, and they are not a
recommendation to buy or sell anything.

- **You can lose money — potentially more than you deposit** — trading leveraged
  FX and CFDs. Most retail accounts lose money.
- **Past performance (including any backtest) does not predict future results.**
  A backtest is a hypothesis, not a promise.
- **The legends are not endorsing this.** Soros, Druckenmiller, Lipschutz,
  Krieger and "TJR" have no connection to this material. We are *studying* their
  publicly documented approaches and synthesising principles, not copying secret
  systems or claiming their returns.
- **The EA is a template, not a money machine.** It is written to be readable and
  teachable. Its default settings are starting points for your own research.
- Nothing here accounts for *your* circumstances. If you need advice, consult a
  licensed professional. Only ever risk money you can afford to lose.

By using this material you accept that all trading decisions, and their
consequences, are yours alone.

## Who this is for

Someone who already understands the basics (what a pip is, how to place an order,
what a candlestick is) and wants a **coherent, rules-based method** with the
discipline that separates the legends from the gamblers.

If you are brand new: open a **demo account** first and spend a few weeks just
watching charts with this framework in mind before risking a cent.

## How to work through it

1. **Read 01 → 07 in order.** They build on each other. Don't skip risk (05).
2. **Mark up live charts** as you read modules 02–04. Find ten historical
   examples of each concept before you trust it.
3. **Study the EA (08)** so the rules stop being abstract.
4. **Backtest and forward-test (09)** — months on demo, not days.
5. **Write your plan (10)** and trade it mechanically. The plan is the product.

## A realistic expectation

You are not going to "break the Bank of England". The goal of this course is far
more boring and far more valuable: to make you **consistent, patient, and small
enough to survive** long enough for asymmetric winners to compound. Survival is
the whole game. Everything else is detail.
