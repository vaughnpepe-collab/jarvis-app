# 01 — The Mindset of Legends

Five operators, five styles, one shared spine. Before any chart pattern, learn
how each of them *thought*. The method in this course is built from the overlap.

## George Soros — reflexivity & the asymmetric jugular

Soros's core idea is **reflexivity**: prices don't just reflect reality, they
*change* it. Rising prices attract buyers and improve fundamentals, which pushes
prices higher still — a feedback loop that builds booms and busts. He hunted for
moments where the crowd's belief and reality were diverging, then bet big on the
gap closing.

His most famous trade — shorting the British pound in 1992 — worked because he
recognised an unsustainable peg (reality vs. policy) and, crucially, **sized the
trade to the conviction**. "When you're right on something, you can't be right
enough." But the other half of the quote matters more: he cut losers fast and
without ego. *Survive small, win enormous.*

**What we take:** trade *with* feedback loops (trends), look for the crowd to be
offside, and when the setup is rare and clean, press it.

## Stanley Druckenmiller — concentration & capital preservation

Druckenmiller, who ran money for Soros, is the patron saint of **asymmetry and
concentration**. His most quoted lesson:

> "It's not whether you're right or wrong that's important, but how much money
> you make when you're right and how much you lose when you're wrong."

He preserved capital ferociously, made his *living* on a handful of huge,
high-conviction macro bets per year, and was happy to do nothing in between. He
also had no loyalty to a position — if the thesis broke, he was out.

**What we take:** few trades, big when they count, ruthless protection of
capital, no emotional attachment to a position.

## Bill Lipschutz — conviction, patience & sizing in FX

Lipschutz traded enormous FX size at Salomon Brothers. His themes:

- **Trade management over entry.** He often built into a position and traded
  *around* a core, scaling as the trade proved itself.
- **Position sizing is everything.** Get the size right and you can be wrong on
  timing and still survive. Get it wrong and one trade ends you.
- **Patience.** A good FX trade can take days to mature; he sat through noise
  when the thesis was intact, and used options/structure to control risk.

**What we take:** a stable core thesis, sizing that lets you withstand
volatility, and the patience to let a correct trade work.

## Andrew Krieger — disciplined aggression

Krieger famously took a vast short position in the New Zealand dollar in 1987,
using options for leverage and conviction. The lesson is **not** "use huge
leverage". It is that when a *specific, rare* opportunity appears, a prepared
trader with a defined risk can press an advantage hard — *deliberately*, not
recklessly. Leverage is a tool that magnifies whatever discipline (or lack of it)
you already have.

**What we take:** aggression is earned by preparation and a defined downside —
never by hope.

## TJR — the modern mechanical edge

"TJR" represents the contemporary **ICT / smart-money** retail style: precise,
mechanical, and obsessed with *where the liquidity is*. The core observations:

- Markets move to **take liquidity** — they run obvious highs/lows where stops
  sit, then reverse.
- A **shift in market structure** after a liquidity grab signals the real move.
- **Timing matters**: trade the active sessions (London, New York) when the
  liquidity is genuine, and stand aside in the dead hours.

**What we take:** the mechanical *trigger* — liquidity sweep → structure shift —
plus session discipline. This is what makes the macro thesis tradeable on a
chart, and what the EA automates.

## The synthesis

| From | Principle we keep |
|---|---|
| Soros | Trade with the tide; press rare, clean setups |
| Druckenmiller | Asymmetry & capital preservation; few big trades |
| Lipschutz | Sizing & patience; manage the trade, not just the entry |
| Krieger | Earned, defined-risk aggression |
| TJR | Mechanical trigger: sweep → shift; session timing |

> **The thesis of this course in one line:** *Macro tide (Druckenmiller/Soros) +
> mechanical liquidity trigger (TJR) + asymmetric risk and ruthless management
> (Lipschutz/Krieger) = a repeatable, survivable edge.*
